Crop Science
UC Davis CropManage research for your specific crops
Strawberry — Irrigation Guidelines
Strawberries in the Salinas Valley require consistent moisture during fruit development. UC Davis recommends drip irrigation with soil moisture monitoring. The recommended interval adjusts based on ET data, soil type, and growth stage. During peak production (March-May), intervals typically range from 3-7 days depending on weather conditions.
Lettuce — Water Management
Head lettuce is sensitive to both over- and under-irrigation. CropManage calculates water needs based on canopy coverage and reference ET. Early growth stages need lighter, more frequent irrigation. As the crop matures, intervals can extend but amounts increase. Monitor for tip burn — often a sign of calcium deficiency linked to inconsistent watering.
Broccoli — Seasonal Notes
Spring broccoli in the central coast benefits from moderate irrigation. CropManage recommendations account for the cooler coastal temperatures. Key period is head formation — consistent moisture is critical. Reduce irrigation as heads mature to prevent hollow stem.
